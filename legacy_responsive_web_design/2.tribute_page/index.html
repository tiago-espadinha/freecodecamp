Jane Goodall

A Life Dedicated to Understanding and Protecting Our Planet

Goodall's lifetime achievements:

  • 1960 – Began her groundbreaking chimpanzee research at Gombe Stream National Park in Tanzania.
  • 1965 – Earned her PhD in Ethology from the University of Cambridge, one of the few people allowed to pursue a doctorate without a bachelor’s degree.
  • 1977 – Founded the Jane Goodall Institute, dedicated to wildlife research, conservation, and education.
  • 1991 – Launched the global youth program Roots & Shoots to empower young people to take action for people, animals, and the environment.
  • 2002 – Appointed UN Messenger of Peace, recognizing her global advocacy for conservation and humanitarian causes.
